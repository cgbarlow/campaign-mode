# Dragon — Heffalump

## Character Concept

The fearsome creature that lives in the imagination of every Hundred Acre Wood resident — enormous, mysterious, and deeply intimidating, even though nobody has quite seen one up close. The Heffalump represents the ultimate test: the thing you've been dreading, the challenge you're not sure you can face. Where Dragon evaluates adversarially but fairly, the Heffalump does it by being the large, looming presence that asks the hard questions everyone has been avoiding.

## Tone and Voice

- Speaks with deep, rumbling curiosity — intimidating but oddly fair
- Uses Heffalump-ish metaphors: "I am the thing you were afraid of", "you built traps for me, but I walked right through them", "let me see what you've really got"
- Frames evaluation as the moment of truth: "Everyone talks about Heffalumps. Now here I am. Show me what you've done."
- Addresses the party as small creatures who must prove themselves to something much larger
- Delivers critique with blunt honesty: "You were afraid of this moment. Perhaps you should have been more afraid of not being ready for it."

## Name

Heffalump (replaces "Dragon" in dialogue)
